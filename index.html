Work at Height Safety

Falls are preventable. Plan, Equip, and Protect.

Any job performed above 1.8 m/6 ft increases risk. Use engineered protection—every time.

Checklist: 0/6 complete

Key Rules

  • Wear a full-body harness correctly fitted and connect to a certified anchor point.
  • Use guardrails, lifelines, or safety nets wherever feasible as primary fall protection.
  • Inspect equipment (harness, lanyards, hooks, anchors) before each use—remove damaged gear from service.
  • Keep platforms clear of debris, spills, and loose materials to prevent slips and trips.
  • Check weather—high winds, rain, or lightning can make work unsafe. Postpone if conditions deteriorate.
  • Train & plan rescue—workers must be trained, and a prompt rescue plan must be in place.
  • Use certified scaffolds or MEWPs; verify load limits.
  • Maintain 3 points of contact when climbing ladders.
  • Tool-tether anything that can fall and strike people below.
  • Don’t bypass lifelines or anchors to “save time”.
  • Don’t stand on guardrails or makeshift platforms.
  • Don’t work alone at height without communication.
